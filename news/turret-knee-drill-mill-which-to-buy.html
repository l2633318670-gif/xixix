Turret Mill vs Knee Mill vs Drill Mill: Which Should You Buy?

The three most popular used milling machines we export from China are turret mills, vertical knee-type mills, and drilling-milling machines. They look similar to beginners, but each suits a different kind of work and budget. Here is a simple guide to help you choose.

Quick comparison

Turret millGeneral workshops, tool rooms, repair shopsFlexible head, angled cuts, easy tooling (R8)4# / X6325
Knee millProduction, heavy steel & cast ironRigid, powerful, large heavy workX5032

1. Turret milling machine

A turret mill (Bridgeport-type) has a swiveling, tilting head and a quill. This makes it very flexible for drilling, boring, and light-to-medium milling, including angled cuts. The R8 spindle uses tooling that is easy to find worldwide. It is the most popular "first milling machine" for general workshops.

Good choices: 4# Turret Mill and the compact X6325 Turret Mill, both with a 2-axis DRO.

2. Vertical knee-type mill

A knee mill such as the X5032 has a heavy cast-iron body and a powerful ISO 50 spindle (7.5 kW). It is built for heavy, continuous milling on steel, cast iron, and large parts. If you run a production workshop, steel fabrication, or agricultural-equipment shop, this is the workhorse to choose. A version with an external control box makes wiring and servicing easier.

3. Drilling-milling machine

A drill mill combines drilling, tapping, and light milling in one compact machine. The ZX50C is ideal for small shops and schools with limited space, while the larger ZX6350 offers more capacity (up to 50 mm drilling, 80 mm face milling) and comes with a DRO. Great value for maintenance teams and traders starting out.

How to decide

  • Limited space or budget? Start with a drill mill (ZX50C / ZX6350).
  • General machining and flexibility? Choose a turret mill (4# / X6325).
  • Heavy steel and production? Go for a knee mill (X5032).
